# Trauma-Informed Patterns

Government forms often ask about difficult life events. Death of a family member, military trauma, domestic violence, financial hardship. CivUI provides a set of patterns that help users navigate these sections with dignity, control, and clear expectations.

These patterns don't change the data collected. They change how it's presented.

## Principles

1. **Set expectations:** tell users what's coming before they see it
2. **Give control:** let users pause, skip, or opt out of specific questions
3. **Don't assume:** avoid redundant or insensitive questions when context is clear
4. **Keep help visible:** crisis resources should be accessible throughout

## Available patterns

### 1. Section intro. Set expectations

`<civ-section-intro>` places a context panel before a sensitive section, explaining what's coming and why.

<StoryEmbed id="forms-patterns-trauma-informed--section-intro" />

```html
<civ-section-intro heading="About your service-connected trauma" tone="sensitive">
  <p>The next questions ask about events that may be difficult to remember.</p>
  <p>You can skip any question, and your answers are saved as you go.</p>
</civ-section-intro>
```

**Tones:**
- `info` (default). Neutral informational context
- `sensitive`: visual and tonal softening for emotionally heavy sections
- `neutral`: minimal styling, just the content

**When to use:** Before any section that asks about death, trauma, abuse, financial hardship, or other sensitive topics.

### 2. Form step sensitive. Pause and resume

Adding `sensitive` to `<civ-form-step>` enables two things:
- A polite screen reader announcement: "This section asks personal questions. Your answers are saved as you go."
- A "Save and come back later" link so users can pause mid-section

<StoryEmbed id="forms-patterns-trauma-informed--form-step-sensitive" />

```html
<civ-form-step sensitive>
  <div data-step-label="About your trauma">
    <!-- sensitive fields -->
  </div>
</civ-form-step>
```

You can also use `show-pause` independently without `sensitive` if you want the pause affordance on any step:

```html
<civ-form-step show-pause>
  <div data-step-label="Financial information">
    <!-- long section users might want to pause -->
  </div>
</civ-form-step>
```

### 3. Skip affordance. Prefer not to answer

`skipLabel` on `<civ-yes-no>` and `<civ-radio-group>` renders a "Prefer not to answer" link-button below the options. This is distinct from "unsure". It's about opting out, not uncertainty.

<StoryEmbed id="forms-patterns-trauma-informed--skip-affordance" />

```html
<civ-yes-no
  legend="Have you experienced military sexual trauma?"
  name="mst"
  skip-label="Prefer not to answer"
  skip-value="skip"
></civ-yes-no>

<civ-radio-group
  legend="What is your race?"
  name="race"
  skip-label="Prefer not to answer"
>
  <civ-radio label="American Indian or Alaska Native" value="aian"></civ-radio>
  <civ-radio label="Asian" value="asian"></civ-radio>
  <civ-radio label="Black or African American" value="black"></civ-radio>
  <civ-radio label="Native Hawaiian or Pacific Islander" value="nhpi"></civ-radio>
  <civ-radio label="White" value="white"></civ-radio>
</civ-radio-group>
```

**When to use:** Demographics, trauma screening, questions about abuse or violence, anything where disclosure is voluntary.

### 4. Deceased assumed. Don't ask what's already known

When a form is explicitly about someone who has died (burial benefits, survivor claims), don't ask "Is this person deceased?" Use `deceased-assumed` on `<civ-relationship>` to skip the yes/no and show the date of death directly.

<StoryEmbed id="forms-patterns-trauma-informed--deceased-assumed" />

```html
<!-- Good: assumes the person is deceased -->
<civ-relationship
  legend="About the person who died"
  preset="survivor"
  deceased-assumed
></civ-relationship>

<!-- Bad: asks a redundant, insensitive question -->
<civ-relationship
  legend="About the person who died"
  preset="survivor"
  show-deceased
></civ-relationship>
```

Use `show-deceased` only when the form doesn't already establish that the person has died. For example, a dependency claim where some dependents may be living and others deceased.

### 5. Support resources. Keep help visible

`supportResources` on `<civ-form>` renders a persistent, non-modal footer with crisis resource links. It stays visible across all form steps.

<StoryEmbed id="forms-patterns-trauma-informed--support-resources" />

```html
<civ-form
  support-resources='[
    {"label": "Veterans Crisis Line", "href": "tel:988", "description": "Call or text 988, then press 1"},
    {"label": "Crisis Text Line", "href": "sms:838255", "description": "Text HOME to 838255"},
    {"label": "Safe Helpline", "href": "tel:18779955247", "description": "For survivors of sexual assault"}
  ]'
>
  <!-- form content -->
</civ-form>
```

URL schemes are restricted to `http`, `https`, `tel`, `mailto`, `sms`, same-origin paths, and hashes.

**When to use:** Any form touching mental health, self-harm risk, domestic violence, sexual trauma, or bereavement.

### 6. Repeater form-steps sensitive. Multi-item sensitive flows

When using `<civ-repeater mode="form-steps">` for sensitive content (e.g., adding multiple deceased dependents), pass `form-steps-sensitive` and `form-steps-show-pause` to enable trauma-informed affordances inside the form-steps sub-flow.

<StoryEmbed id="forms-patterns-trauma-informed--repeater-form-steps-sensitive" />

```html
<civ-repeater
  legend="People who died"
  name="deceased"
  item-label="person"
  mode="form-steps"
  form-steps-sensitive
  form-steps-show-pause
>
  <div data-step-label="Their name">
    <civ-name legend="Their name" name="name" required></civ-name>
  </div>
  <div data-step-label="Relationship">
    <civ-relationship
      legend="Your relationship"
      preset="survivor"
      deceased-assumed
      name="rel"
      required
      hide-name
    ></civ-relationship>
  </div>
</civ-repeater>
```

Without these props, the form-steps sub-flow has no pause affordance or sensitive announcements.

## Complete example: VA survivor benefit form

This example combines all trauma-informed patterns into a realistic VA burial benefit application:

<StoryEmbed id="forms-patterns-trauma-informed--complete-survivor-form" />

```html
<civ-form
  support-resources='[
    {"label": "Veterans Crisis Line", "href": "tel:988"},
    {"label": "Caregiver Support", "href": "tel:18552603274"}
  ]'
  @civ-submit="${handleSubmit}"
>
  <civ-form-step>
    <!-- Step 1: Non-sensitive, no special handling needed -->
    <div data-step-label="Your information">
      <civ-name legend="Your name" name="claimant" required></civ-name>
      <civ-text-input label="Phone number" name="phone" type="tel"></civ-text-input>
    </div>

    <!-- Step 2: Sensitive — about the deceased -->
    <div data-step-label="About the person who died">
      <civ-section-intro heading="About the person who died" tone="sensitive">
        <p>We need some information about the Veteran who died to process your claim.</p>
        <p>You can save your progress and come back later at any time.</p>
      </civ-section-intro>

      <civ-relationship
        legend="About the Veteran"
        name="veteran"
        preset="survivor"
        deceased-assumed
        required
      ></civ-relationship>
    </div>

    <!-- Step 3: Sensitive — relationship to the deceased -->
    <div data-step-label="Your relationship">
      <civ-yes-no
        legend="Were you legally married at the time of their death?"
        name="marriedAtDeath"
        skip-label="Prefer not to answer"
        required
      ></civ-yes-no>

      <civ-conditional when="marriedAtDeath" equals="yes">
        <civ-partnership-history
          legend="About your marriage"
          name="marriage"
        ></civ-partnership-history>
      </civ-conditional>
    </div>

    <!-- Step 4: Review -->
    <div data-step-label="Review">
      <civ-summary></civ-summary>
    </div>
  </civ-form-step>

  <civ-button type="submit" label="Submit application"></civ-button>
</civ-form>
```

## When to apply these patterns

| Situation | Patterns to use |
|-----------|----------------|
| Form asks about death or bereavement | `section-intro sensitive`, `deceased-assumed`, `supportResources` |
| Form asks about trauma or abuse | `section-intro sensitive`, `form-step sensitive`, `skipLabel`, `supportResources` |
| Form asks demographic questions | `skipLabel` on each question |
| Form is long and emotionally draining | `form-step show-pause`, `supportResources` |
| Repeater collects sensitive items | `form-steps-sensitive`, `form-steps-show-pause` |
| Form context already establishes a fact | `deceased-assumed` (don't re-ask what's known) |
