# Multi-Step Form

Break long government forms into manageable steps. Each step covers one concept (not one field). Group related inputs together to reduce page count without increasing cognitive load.

## Live example

<StoryEmbed id="forms-form-form-step--government-benefit-application" />

## How it works

1. Wrap each step's fields in a container with a `data-step-label` attribute
2. `civ-form-step` handles navigation (back/continue), validation, and progress tracking
3. `civ-progress` shows the user where they are

```html
<civ-form-step>
  <div data-step-label="Personal information">
    <civ-name legend="Your name" name="fullName" required></civ-name>
    <civ-memorable-date legend="Date of birth" required name="dob"></civ-memorable-date>
  </div>

  <div data-step-label="Contact information">
    <civ-address legend="Mailing address" name="address" required></civ-address>
    <civ-phone name="phone" required></civ-phone>
    <civ-email name="email" required></civ-email>
  </div>

  <div data-step-label="Review">
    <civ-summary heading="Review your information"></civ-summary>
    <civ-signature legend="Statement of truth" name="signature" required></civ-signature>
  </div>
</civ-form-step>
```

## Progress indicators

Three modes are available via the `progress` attribute. See all three side by side:

<StoryEmbed id="forms-form-form-step--all-progress-modes" />

Use `progress="steps"` for a known step count with section labels:

<StoryEmbed id="forms-form-form-step--with-segmented-steps" />

Use `progress="bar"` for a dynamic / unknown step count where a percentage reads better than circles:

<StoryEmbed id="forms-form-form-step--with-progress-bar" />

The default (`progress="minimal"`, shown above as the first example) is just "Step N of M" text. Appropriate for short forms where a visual progress indicator would feel heavy.

## Step validation

Each step validates its fields before allowing navigation to the next step. Invalid fields show errors and the step doesn't advance.

<StoryEmbed id="forms-form-form-step--with-error" />

## Sensitive steps with pause

For emotionally difficult sections, mark the step as `sensitive`. This adds a polite screen reader announcement on step entry and auto-enables the "Save and come back later" action:

<StoryEmbed id="forms-form-form-step--sensitive" />

You can also opt into the pause affordance alone (without the trauma-informed announcement) by setting `show-pause` directly. Useful for long but not emotionally heavy sections that users might want to save and resume:

<StoryEmbed id="forms-form-form-step--with-pause-and-resume" />

## Grouping guidance

| Grouping | Example fields | Why |
|----------|---------------|-----|
| Name + DOB | First, middle, last, suffix, date of birth | One identity concept |
| Contact | Address, phone, email | One reachability concept |
| Service | Branch, dates, discharge | One service period |
| Review + Sign | Summary, signature, submit | One final confirmation |

Avoid one-field-per-step. It creates 20+ page forms with high abandonment. Group by topic, not by field.
